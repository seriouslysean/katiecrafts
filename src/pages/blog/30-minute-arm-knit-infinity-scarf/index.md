<p>Project: 30-Minute Arm Knit Infinity Scarf</p><p>I have recently learned to…. KNIT! It’s very exciting for me, because I’ve been a self-taught crocheter for about ten years but never so much as picked up knitting needles. Even though every Thursday evening for the last 7-8 months, I’ve gone to a Knitting Group. I know. How could I wait that long to learn? Well, I had a lot of projects to crochet first!</p><p>Finally, when the rush of holiday projects was over, my friend Christine told me it was TIME to learn to knit! She taught me a couple basics and I’m in the midst of my first project right now. Because I was so excited to learn, I thought I’d try to tackle arm knitting (which previously scared me) and this was the result!</p><p>I love this scarf so much! It’s made with just two skeins of chunky/bulky yarn and your arms, so the materials needed to make it are few. I tried several tutorials that I just didn’t quite get until I found this awesome one by</p><a title="Handimania" href="http://www.handimania.com/knit/30-minute-infinity-scarf.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Handimania</p></strong></a><p>. It was very easy to follow and within a half hour I had my favorite scarf to date! Check out their video below and try it out! It’s the perfect weekend project, since it takes less than an hour. 😉</p><p>The photo I took may look like a small-ish infinity cowl, but it’s not at all. It’s very long! But I like it that way. It’s pretty awesome. I encourage you to give arm knitting a go. If you do, share pics in the comments of your projects! I’d love to see them!</p>
