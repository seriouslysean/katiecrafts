<p>Project: DIY Sharpie Mug</p><p>Need a last minute gift for a co-worker before tomorrow? Grab an unused plain coffee cup and a few Sharpie markers and make your friend a personalized mug for their desk! Bonus: Since it’s homemade just for them, they’ll feel extra special and not at all think that you scraped it together last minute.</p><p>I made everyone a personalized mug for Christmas one year, filling them with hot chocolate, marshmallows, candy canes and scratch off tickets. It took me awhile, since I was making over a dozen, but they came out really cute! I vowed to remember the easy DIY for future use, and now I’ll share it with you!</p><h2>Materials:</h2><ul><li><p>Ceramic coffee cup that can withstand heat (should say on the bottom of the mug “oven safe”)</p></li><li><p>Sharpie markers (or another permanent brand) in various colors</p></li><li><p>Oven</p></li><li><p>Cookie sheet</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>Pre-heat your oven to</p><strong><p>350º Fahrenheit.</p></strong></li><li><p>While your oven is heating up, use your</p><a title="Sharpie Markers on Amazon" href="http://amzn.to/1fs7rty" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Sharpie markers</p></strong></a><p>to decorate the mug! You can write “Happy Birthday, Sean!” on it, or “Happy Easter, Kelly!” Whatever your heart desires. Nix wording all together and do a pretty intricate design if that’s what you’re in to. I love brown and blue together and I super love polka dots, so that’s what I went with. 🙂</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" alt="DIY Sharpie Mug by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" src="./_images/img-1364.jpg" width="637" height="849"></p><ul><li><p>When finished, put the mug on a cookie sheet and place in oven for</p><strong><p>30 minutes</p></strong><p>to “cure” it.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" alt="DIY Sharpie Mug by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" src="./_images/img-1365.jpg" width="637" height="345"></p><ul><li><p>After the half hour of baking, carefully remove the hot cookie sheet and let it cool</p><strong><p>completely</p></strong><p>.</p></li><li><p>Once it’s cooled, it’s ready for use.</p></li><li><p>Make sure to tell the receiver of this awesome gift that it’s hand wash</p><strong><p>ONLY</p></strong><p>(dishwasher may take off the design), and not to use anything crazy like a steel wool pad that will surely scrub off the marker. Otherwise, your design will hold up pretty well!</p></li></ul><p>I know you guys will come up with fun designs for this easy project! Share them if you like!</p>
