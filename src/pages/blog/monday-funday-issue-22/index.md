<h2>Project That Inspires: Bubbles!</h2>
<p>As a kid, one of my favorite thing to do was blow bubbles. I still enjoy blowing bubbles today, whether it’s the soapy kind or bubblegum kind! There is just something about watching a bubble form. I was thinking of buying some for my little cousin’s upcoming birthday party as a present, and then thought, “Wait! For once I can do a home craft!” Making homemade bubbles is super easy to do and it doesn’t really cost you too much. I looked online and found this simple way to make bubbles on <a title="ehow" href="http://www.ehow.com/how_4476827_homemade-bubbles-kids.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">eHow.com</a> and I think I am gonna try it. Here’s what you need to make them:</p>
<ul>
<li>Something big to put the bubbles in, like a bucket</li>
<li>1 cup dish soap (like Dawn)</li>
<li>1/4 cup corn syrup (which apparently makes the solution stronger so that the bubble’s don’t pop)</li>
<li>4 cups of water</li>
</ul>
<p>Pour the 4 cups of water into your container. The next step is to slowly add the corn syrup and dish soap at the same time to the water. Then, allow the mixture to sit for 30 minutes so it can mix well together. Lastly, just pour it into something for easier dipping and enjoy.</p>
