<p>Mother’s Day is a tough day for me, having lost mine 3 and a half years ago to brain cancer (</p><strong><a href="/celebrating-mom/">learn more about my mom here.</a></strong><p>) I’m glad, though, that it falls when it does. May is Brain Tumor Awareness Month!</p><p>Learn more about brain tumors and find ways to take action at the</p><a href="http://braintumor.org/join-the-fight/brain-tumor-awareness-month/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>National Brain Tumor Society’s website</p></strong></a><p>.</p><p>You can also join my</p><a href="http://www.braintumorcommunity.org/site/TR/TeamraiserEvents/RaceforHopePhiladelphia?px=2774473&#x26;pg=personal&#x26;fr_id=2740" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Race For Hope Philly Team</p></strong></a><p>(aptly named</p><a href="https://www.facebook.com/givekathyhope/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Give Kathy Hope</p></strong></a><p>, after mom) and be a walker/runner, virtual walker, volunteer, or you can donate! The race this year is Sunday, October 2, 2016 at The Navy Yard, Marine Parade Grounds in Philadelphia, PA.</p><p>We can’t believe it’s the 7th Race For Hope we’ll be participating in!! She may be gone, but she will never be forgotten!</p><p>I’ll be using the hashtag #BTAM for the rest of May to help spread the word! Don’t forget to do the same!</p><p>Are you participating in any of the Races For Hope across the US? Tell me about them in the comments!</p>
