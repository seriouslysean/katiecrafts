<strong><br></strong><p>Inspired yesterday by</p><em><a title="The Art and Tree Chatter of Aquariann" href="http://blog.aquariann.com/2014/08/french-tip-manicure-watermelon-nail-art.html" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Aquariann</a><p>‘s</p></em><p>watermelon nail wrap post, I decided to try my hand at making my own watermelon nail art design! My</p><em><a title="Apples Nail Art Design" href="/apples-nail-art-design/">apple design</a></em><p>is still my fave fruit so far, but these are a pretty cute way to end the summer. BONUS: If you skip one step, you end up with</p><em><p>strawberry nails</p></em><p>! Which do you like better?</p><h2>Materials:</h2><ul><li><p>Pink or red nail polish</p></li><li><p>Green nail polish</p></li><li><p>Black nail polish</p></li><li><p>White striper or white nail polish + nail art brush</p></li><li><p>Dotting tool or toothpick</p></li><li><p>Clear top coat</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>With clean dry nails, do one coat of your pink or red nail polish and let dry. I used my favorite coral shade (</p><a title="Essie " come="" here!&#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x22;="" polish&#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x22;="" href="http://amzn.to/1n5xxBU" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Essie’s “Come Here”</a><p>) since soon I will have to retire it for the season! You don’t have to use coral, if you don’t want to, though! You can use red if you are going for a strawberry look, or hot pink for a great watermelon design!</p></li></ul><p>One streaky coat! (Please ignore how horribly cracked and dry my hands look! No matter now much lotion I put on them before I take photos for the blog, they always look like they belong to someone 108 years old.)</p><ul><li><p>See those streak marks from one coat? Do a second coat and let dry completely.</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_4075" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4075" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-5245.jpg" alt="Watermelon Nail Art Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="771"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4075"><p>Two coats- much better!</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>With your green polish (I used Confetti’s “My Favorite Martian”), do a somewhat thick french tip on the top quarter to third of your nail. This is the top of your watermelon slice! Let dry.</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_4076" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4076" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-5247.jpg" alt="Watermelon Nail Art Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="784"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4076"><p>One coat of green</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>If the polish you choose is streaky here too, do a second green tip coat and let dry.</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_4077" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4077" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-5249.jpg" alt="Watermelon Nail Art Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="784"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4077"><p>Two coats of green</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>Use your dotting tool or toothpick and some black nail polish, and make little seeds throughout the pink/red. Let dry.</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_4078" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4078" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-5251.jpg" alt="Watermelon Nail Art Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="706"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4078"><p>Seeds!</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>Right now, you can call it a day, do a quick coat of clear polish, and end the design with some strawberry nails if you want!</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_4079" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4079" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-5256.jpg" alt="Watermelon Nail Art Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="677"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4079"><p>Don’t worry about nails not being cleaned up- you can do this at the end!</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>If you want to continue to have watermelon nails, use your white striper or white nail polish and a nail art brush to make a thin line between where the pink and green meet.</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_4081" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-4081" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-5262.jpg" alt="Watermelon Nail Art Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="800"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-4081"><p>Watermelons!</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>Finish with clear top coat. Done!</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
