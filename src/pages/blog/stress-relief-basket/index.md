<p>Project: Stress Relief Basket (+ Lavender Bath Salt Tutorial!)</p><p>On Sunday, we celebrated my oldest friend, Krystal, at her bridal shower! I put together some decorations, the centerpieces and a few extras (which I’ll show throughout the week!) I know how stressed I was in the month before my wedding, so I wanted to help her out with a basket filled with stress relieving goodies!</p><p>I really wanted to pull together things that would make Krystal feel better, or at least forget about wedding-stress-blues momentarily over the next few weeks as her big day approaches. It took me a few days to figure out what I wanted to put in the basket, but I think it came out pretty well!</p><p>This basket is less of a how-to project and more of a this-is-what-I-bought-to-put-in-the-basket. I DID make the bath salt though, so I’ll tell you how to make that!</p><p>First, let’s talk about how cute this basket is. It was one of those really awesome Marshalls finds that happens occasionally! If there were two, I would have picked one up for myself as well. The pattern, the material. I just love it. It’s so pretty!</p><p><img class="size-medium post__image" alt="Stress Relief Basket on Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" src="./_images/stress-relief-basket-1.jpg" width="637" height="486"></p><h2>Contents of the basket…</h2><ul><li><p>Two adorable books that are easy to read and fluffy to take her mind off drama! The first is</p><a title="The Wedding Girl by Madeliene Wickham" href="http://amzn.to/1nF71EN" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">“The Wedding Girl” by Madeleine Wickham</a><p>. The second is</p><a title="I Lived, I Lost, I Made Spaghetti by Giulia Melucci" href="http://amzn.to/1enylkH" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">“I Loved, I Lost, I Made Spaghetti” by Giulia Melucci</a><p>. Both are great reads that I definitely recommend!</p></li><li><p>A cute little mug for tea, with little birds on it (she’s obsessed with birds!)</p></li><li><p>A box of English Breakfast Tea to sip while reading</p></li><li><p>Some Lindt chocolate truffles, because, um, chocolate!</p></li><li><p>A little candle, scented like fresh linen (my favorite!)</p></li><li><p>Bubble wrap to pop when stressed</p></li><li><p>Lavender bath salts for a soothing bath</p></li><li><p>A gigantic bottle of wine for when all else fails!</p></li></ul><p>I decorated a sheet of paper with a list of the items in the basket and a little love note and rolled it up scroll-style to stick inside.</p><p><img class="size-medium post__image" alt="Stress Relief Basket on Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" src="./_images/stress-relief-basket-3.jpg" width="637" height="444"></p><p>Now on to the lavender bath salt tutorial! This is SO EASY. I didn’t take pictures of it, but you really don’t need any. Just follow the instructions below!</p><h2>Materials:</h2><ul><li><p>Epson Salt, 2 parts</p></li><li><p>Course Sea Salt, 1 part</p></li><li><p>Dried Lavender, 1 or 2 tsp(s)</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>Mix both salts and dried lavender together. Stick in mason jar. BOOM!</p></li><li><p>To use, sprinkle in warm bath water. Relax and enjoy!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" alt="Stress Relief Basket on Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" src="./_images/stress-relief-basket-4.jpg" width="637" height="477"></p><p>I hope the basket brings my darling friend a few minutes of peace in the weeks to come! What would you put in your stress relief basket?</p>
