<p><em>?On the eighth day of Christmas, Katie Crafts gave to me…?</em></p><p>A fantastic handmade gift idea for the holiday season! This DIY 2 ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap tutorial is so easy you’ll be whipping up homemade soaps for everyone in your family this holiday season! But don’t forget to keep some for yourself!</p><h2>Ingredients:</h2><ul><li><a href="http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/B002PNV3DI/ref=as_li_qf_sp_asin_il_tl?ie=UTF8&#x26;camp=1789&#x26;creative=9325&#x26;creativeASIN=B002PNV3DI&#x26;linkCode=as2&#x26;tag=katicraf-20&#x26;linkId=GTBQJH3LKT3EAN76" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Goat’s Milk Soap</a></li><li><p>Raw Unfiltered Honey</p></li><li><p>Yellow Dye (optional)</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>Cut 1/4 (which is about a half pound) of the Goat’s Milk Soap into cubes with a knife. It’s soft so it will cut pretty easily. This will yield three large bars of soap!</p></li></ul><ul><li><p>Use a metal spatula or something similar to help you left the cubes out of the packaging and put them into the glass measuring cup. I accomplished this with a pie server!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-full post__image" src="./_images/img-4607.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="980" height="980"></p><ul><li><p>Microwave for 30 seconds. Stir.</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-4608.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-4610.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Repeat the microwave-30-seconds-&#x26;-stir step until everything is melted and liquid. This usually is about 3 to 4 intervals.</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-4611.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-4612.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>While it’s still hot, add 2 Tablespoons of Raw Unfiltered Honey to the soap. Stir until completely mixed.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-full post__image" src="./_images/img-4613.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="980" height="980"></p><ul><li><p>I also stir in three drops of yellow coloring to make it a little more “honey” like in color, but this is completely optional.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-full post__image" src="./_images/img-4614.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="980" height="980"></p><ul><li><p>Pour into soap molds and let set in freezer for an hour.</p></li><li><p>Remove molds from freezer, turn over and gently pop out the soap bars. If they are still frozen to the molds, use your hand to warm them up for a minute and try again. They should come out fairly easily.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-full post__image" src="./_images/img-4624.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="980" height="980"></p><ul><li><p>That’s it! Enjoy your milk and honey soap!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-full post__image" src="./_images/img-4621.jpg" alt="DIY 2 Ingredient Milk &#x26; Honey Soap Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="980" height="980"></p><p>What do you think of my super easy DIY soap recipe? If you decide to use it for a quick holiday gift idea, share a pic in the comments!</p>
