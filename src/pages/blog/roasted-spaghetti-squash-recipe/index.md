<p>My Whole30 Challenge is complete, and I’m just in the midst of reintroducing each forbidden food now to see what my body can handle and what it can’t. During this time, I have to stick to compliant recipes like this roasted spaghetti squash one! The Husband REALLY loved it, so I know I’ll be making it again soon- diet or not!</p><p>I’ve never made a spaghetti squash before, so I wasn’t sure how to go about it. I was especially scared to try to cut that thing open. It’s really heavy and hard! Thankfully, I found that roasting it first makes the whole cutting part easier, so I thought I’d share just what I did with you guys.</p><p><em>*Disclaimer: You are going to want this to taste like spaghetti because of it’s name and appearance. It doesn’t! It’s still a squash! But it’s definitely worth a try!*</em></p><h2>Ingredients:</h2><ul><li><p>Medium sized spaghetti squash</p></li><li><a href="/how-to-make-your-own-ghee/">Clarified butter or ghee</a><p>*</p></li><li><p>Paleo approved bacon (I buy mine at Whole Foods!)*</p></li><li><p>Salt and pepper to taste</p></li></ul><p>*If you aren’t doing Whole30 or Paleo diets, feel free to use regular butter and bacon!</p><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>Pre-heat your oven to 400º F.</p></li><li><p>Wash the outside of your squash and place directly on the oven rack.</p></li><li><p>Bake for one hour, or until squash is golden and a fork punctures the skin easily.</p></li><li><p>Take out using oven mitts and let cool while you prepare bacon.</p></li><li><p>Cook bacon according to package. Set aside.</p></li><li><p>Your squash may still be HOT HOT HOT, so be careful! Use a large knife to cut the squash in half lengthwise.</p></li><li><p>Use a spoon and fork to remove the seeds and “guts,” while leaving the spaghetti like insides alone.</p></li><li><p>Use fork to scrape all the noodle-like bits into a bowl.</p></li><li><p>Top with crumbled bacon, clarified butter, and salt and pepper to taste.</p></li><li><p>Enjoy as a tasty little lunch or an even better side dish!</p></li></ul><p><em>Pro Tip: You can keep the seeds and roast them as you would pumpkin seeds, if you like! However, there aren’t a lot of them, so it may be more work for not a huge reward.</em></p><p>What is your favorite spaghetti squash recipe? Share below!</p>
