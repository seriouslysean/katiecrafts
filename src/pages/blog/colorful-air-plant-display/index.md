<p>I mentioned that I bought some plants at the</p><a title="2014 Philadelphia Flower Show Recap!" href="/2014-philadelphia-flower-show-recap/">Flower Show</a><p>the other day. Well, they weren’t just any plants. They were air plants, that are super cool and require little attention and can be mounted on my wall in weird test tube type glass containers. So it’s basically true love.</p><p>We stopped by all the vendor booths at the Philly Flower Show, but for the second year in a row,</p><a title="Chive" href="https://www.chive.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Chive</p></strong></a><p>is the only one that held my attention. Their array of unique terrariums, tubes, pots and the like really stand out against the rest. Seriously, go check them out!</p><p>If you’re still wondering what “air plants” are, here’s a simple explanation: Air plants, also known as Tillandsia, don’t need soil. They don’t need bright light. They don’t need daily waterings. They are a low maintenance form of succulents- which are already pretty low maintenance as it is. You just give them a good soaking once a week, let them dry out upside down, and place them back on top of their mossy little home. They are also totally perfect for making living wall art with!</p><p>We picked up two Wall Tubes to mount on the wall above my new crafting table, and all the makings for new living wall art right from the Chive booth. As soon as I got home, I put them together.</p><h2>Materials Used For This Project:</h2><ul><li><p>Wall Tubes in varying sizes</p></li><li><p>Turquoise blue sand</p></li><li><p>Lime green sand</p></li><li><p>Clear quartz-like rocks</p></li><li><p>Yellow moss</p></li><li><p>Two different air plants</p></li><li><p>A little piggy and a little tiger!</p></li></ul><p>The instructions are too easy for me to even list for you. Just pour the ingredients in one by one, layering them sand-rocks-moss. Place plant on top. Finish with little rubber animal. Enjoy your new plant. Need/want photos of the process?
