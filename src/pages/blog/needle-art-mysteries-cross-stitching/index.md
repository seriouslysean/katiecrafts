What a perfect post for the occasion!</p></blockquote><p>Hey guys, it’s Jess here with another round of needle art mysteries revealed! I know that I said in my</p><a title="Needle Art Mysteries" href="/needle-art-mysteries-sewing/">last issue</a><p>that I would be tackling knitting, however I found this needle art and thought it might be a good one to continue on from sewing. It’s the needle art of cross-stitching!</p><h2>Cross-Stitching</h2><p>This needle art is one of the forms of embroidery (which I will be covering more about in another post) that you can do. This art is slightly different than sewing in that you follow a set pattern. There are two forms that you can do.</p><ol><li><em><p>Counted Cross-stitch</p></em><p>is when the pattern is on a piece of paper. In order to do this form, you must count the number of squares on the paper and then on your cloth that you will be using. This form is a bit more difficult to do.</p></li><li><p>The other form is called</p><em><p>Stamp Cross-stitching</p></em><p>, in which the pattern is imprinted right on the cloth for you. It is a much easier process to do.</p></li></ol><p>Both forms use a needle, embroidery thread, and the evenweave fabric called aida cloth.</p><p>Katie, starting out a cross-stitch beach pattern for her mother-in-law’s birthday gift</p><p>Cross-stitching is one of the oldest forms of embroidery out there. There are artworks found all throughout the world. In folk museums that can be found in some parts of Europe and Asia, they still showcase many beautiful works of art. They used cross-stitching as a way to teach young girls the alphabet, by having them stitch it! It was also said that it would help the young women be prepared when they had to do household sewing.</p><p>Cross-stitching as defined by</p><a title="Cross Stitch" href="http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/cross-stitch" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Merriam Webster Dictionary</a><p>Online is as follows: “A needlework stitch that forms an X.” You can’t get more succinct than that, right!?</p><figure id="attachment_3758" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-3758" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-4822.jpg" alt="Needle Art Mysteries: Cross-Stitching on Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="663" height="497"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-3758"><p>Katie’s cross-stitch, completed, over 30 hours of work later!</p></figcaption></figure><p>Hope you learned something new about this needle art mystery! I know I did! See ya next time! 🙂</p>
