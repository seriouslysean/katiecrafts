<p>This week’s nail art design is perfect for Easter Sunday! It incorporates little speckled Easter eggs and a little lattice/basket weave accent nail with flowers. They are simple and easy and were fun to paint (even if they were a little hard to do on my left hand!)</p><h2>Materials:</h2><ul><li><p>Clear base coat</p></li><li><p>Sheer pink transparent nail polish</p></li><li><p>Lime or bright green nail polish</p></li><li><p>Orchid or lavender nail polish</p></li><li><p>Blue nail polish</p></li><li><p>White striper</p></li><li><p>Dotting tools/bobby pins/toothpicks</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>Start out with clean dry nails.</p></li><li><p>Do a coat of clear base coat and let it dry.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-2432.jpg" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="805"></p><ul><li><p>Once dry, you’ll paint your first coat of the sheer pink transparent nail polish on. I picked the lightest shade from my</p><a title="Physician&#x27;s Formula Trio: In The Nude" href="http://amzn.to/1peWyRe" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Physician’s Formula Trio “In The Nude.”</a><p>It’s a great set!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-2435.jpg" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="691"></p><ul><li><p>After a first coat is dry, do a second if your polish is streaky. I may have even gone for a third with this one, but I was getting too impatient! You can see below that a second coat wasn’t even too noticeable!</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/sheer-nails-1-coat.png" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/two-coats-sheer.png" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Next you can do your accent nail. I used a white striper to draw a criss-cross basket weave/lattice design on my ring fingers.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/img-2437.jpg" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="679"></p><ul><li><p>Next, I used my dotting tool to make two flowers on each accent nail. I made five dots for the bigger flower, and four for the smaller. Use whatever colors you like for the petals! Just be sure the center of each flower is in a contrasting color!</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2438.jpg" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2439.jpg" alt="Nail Art: Easter Design by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Now you can move on to the eggs! Make different sized, different colored eggs using your dotting tools on each nail. Play around with them! Put them wherever you like!</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
