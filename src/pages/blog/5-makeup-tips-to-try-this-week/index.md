<p>My aunt and uncle gave me a $50 gift card for Christmas that I’ve been holding on to for something fun. When</p><a title="Eyes Lips Face" href="http://www.eyeslipsface.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>e.l.f. (eyes lips face)</p></strong></a><p>had a 30% off everything Tax Day sale, I knew it was time to get shopping! My AMAZING makeup haul arrived yesterday and I can’t wait to dig in. I want to learn more about makeup in general, and am particularly excited to try out these 5 tips and tutorials.</p><p>My eyes never look very open, and I never realized I could fake their size by using makeup. I know you can create illusions using tips and tricks for other things, but it never occurred to me to try it with makeup to open up my eyes. I love</p><a title="Stephanie Lange YouTube" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J4lfQLgBWrY" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Stephanie Lange</p></strong></a><p>‘s channel, especially her tutorials on creating a cut crease.</p><br><p><br></p><p>I already like to wear winged liquid eyeliner on a daily basis, but I bought a few pots of cream eyeliner to try out. I love</p><a title="Selina Lundstrom YouTube" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dVoD850wROc" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Selina Lundstrom</p></strong></a><p>‘s tips on using a cream eyeliner to create a winged look. She also shares how to use other types of eyeliner too, so it’s helpful all around!</p><br><p><br></p><p>I’m really interested in learning how to contour and highlight and picked up some powder and cream bronzers and highlighters to try it out. I like this tutorial from</p><a title="Lauren Curtis YouTube" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lZLwiOqpUfQ" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Lauren Curtis</p></strong></a><p>because she doesn’t just show you how to contour and highlight, she explains what they are and why you’d use them.</p><br><p><br></p><p>One thing I’ve been trying to work on for awhile now but haven’t quite got the hang of yet is foundation and concealer. No matter what I do, it always just looks dry on my skin- whether I use powder, liquid or a combo of both. It’s probably because I have no idea how to apply it properly. That’s why I love</p><a title="Jaclyn Hill YouTube" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KOcbn1B-srM" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Jaclyn Hill</p></strong></a><p>‘s video!</p><br><p><br></p><p>One last thing I need to start learning is how to do makeup when you wear glasses! A lot of makeup tutorials I love look wonderful when I try them but get lost beneath my glasses.</p><strong><p>Sinead</p></strong><p>from</p><a title="The Makeup Chair on YouTube" href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=u1378wjM47M" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>The Makeup Chair</p></strong></a><p>has some great tips!</p><br><p><br></p><p>I’ll do a few product reviews as I test out the ridiculous haul I bought (I’m not kidding- there are over 25 items in it!) and possibly a giveaway of a product I really loved, too! Stay tuned!</p><p>What does your daily makeup routine look like? Any tips you’d love to share?</p><blockquote><p><a href="http://www.freepik.com/free-photos-vectors/hand" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><em>Hand vector designed by Freepik</em></a></p></blockquote>
