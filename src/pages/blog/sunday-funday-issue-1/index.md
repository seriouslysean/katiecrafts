<p>Happy Sunday!</p><p>Each Sunday, I will have a</p><strong><p>“Sunday Funday”</p></strong><p>post, where I fill in my favorite things for each week in five categories.</p><p>They’ll be flexible, but they’ll basically be as follows:</p><ol><li><strong><p>Makes Me Laugh</p></strong><p>– (think YouTube and think cat videos… probably for most of the time!)</p></li><li><strong><p>What I’m Reading</p></strong><p>– a book, article or snippet I read this week that I loved</p></li><li><strong><p>Place I Love</p></strong><p>– a place, past or present, that I’ve visited and adored</p></li><li><strong><p>Something Delicious</p></strong><p>– something completely tasty that I either ate this week, or found the recipe for and cannot wait to try making</p></li><li><strong><p>Project That Inspires</p></strong><p>– this will be a project, craft or the like that I’ve found and draw inspiration from. Maybe it will be something I try in the future! If you have ideas for this or want one of your projects to be considered (and your blog linked to it), just send me an email!</p></li></ol><p>Without further ado, let us get on with</p><strong><p>Sunday Funday: Issue 1!</p></strong><h2>Makes Me Laugh: Smitten – Simon’s Cat (A Valentine’s Special)</h2><p>Ahhh</p><a title="Simon&#x27;s Cat" href="http://www.youtube.com/user/simonscat?feature=watch" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Simon’s Cat</p></strong></a><p>! How I love you. A new Valentine’s short was posted this week that was adorable, so I must share it with you!</p><br><h2>What I’m Reading: “Meet Me at the Cupcake Café” by Jenny Colgan</h2><p>Right now, I’m smack in the middle of a cute book by</p><strong><p>Jenny Colgan</p></strong><p>called</p><a title="" meet="" me="" at="" the="" cupcake="" café&#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x22;="" by="" jenny="" colgan&#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x26;#x22;="" href="http://amzn.to/1erpp9m" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>“Meet Me at the Cupcake Café.”</p></strong></a><p>It’s about a woman who is passionate for baking and decides to open a café after being laid off her desk job. It’s super cute and fluffy- just the type of reading I’m in to lately. Anything that’s heavy or makes me cry just isn’t in the cards right now.</p><strong><p>Bonus:</p></strong><p>Included before every chapter of this book is a delicious recipe! I’ll certainly be trying some out.</p><h2>Place I Love: Plenty Café!</h2><p><a title="Plenty Cafe Philadelphia" href="http://www.plentyphiladelphia.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Plenty</strong></a></p><p>is my absolute favorite coffee shop here in Rittenhouse, and conveniently only a block away. The Husband (in the black coat standing at the counter!) and I are obsessed and go there many, many times a week. Sometimes we bring along our books to read or our laptops to work. It’s lovely.</p><p><a href="http://www.plentyphiladelphia.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><img class="post__image" alt="Plenty Café, Rittenhouse" src="./_images/img-0433.jpg" width="573" height="764"></a></p><h2>Something Delicious: Café Mocha &#x26; Lemon Macaron!</h2><p><a title="Plenty Cafe Philadelphia" href="http://www.plentyphiladelphia.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong>Plenty</strong></a></p><p>(in addition to amazing food and being one of my favorite places to visit) has the best café mocha</p><strong><p>ever</p></strong><p>(made with homemade chocolate!) and this week they also happened to have macarons, too!
