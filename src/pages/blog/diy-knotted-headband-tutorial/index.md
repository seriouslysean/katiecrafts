<p>Project: DIY Knotted Headband</p><p>I LOVE this headband! I have made several different colors of it already out of the</p><a title="How To Make T-Shirt Yarn" href="/how-to-make-t-shirt-yarn/">t-shirt yarn</a><p>that you already know I have a small obsession with (thumbs up for upcycling!) For awhile, I was just tying the ends to a hair tie, like I did in the no-sew version of</p><a title="No Sew Braided Headband" href="/no-sew-braided-headband/">my braided headband</a><p>, but I recently bought some fold-over elastic and decided to add it in to the mix. I’m liking the way it came out!</p><h2>Materials:</h2><ul><li><p>4 strips of t-shirt yarn (enough to wrap around your head)</p></li><li><p>Fold over elastic (4 – 6 inches worth)</p></li><li><p>Sewing machine and matching yarn and/or thread and needle</p></li><li><p>Pins</p></li><li><p>Scissors</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><ul><li><p>Measure your head! See how much yarn you’ll need to fit a snug headband. Add about two inches to it, as you’ll lose a little length when you sew and snip excess off.</p></li></ul><ul><li><p>Place together two strips each. We’ll refer to the first set as “String Set 1” or “First String Set” and the second set as “U Set.”</p></li><li><p>With First string set, make a ribbon shape, with a loop on top. Make sure the bottom tails are</p><strong><p>exactly</p></strong><p>the way as pictured below.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-2.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="775"></p><ul><li><p>Next, take your second set of strings and make a</p><strong><p>“U”</p></strong><p>shape on</p><strong><p>top</p></strong><p>of the first set of strings.</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-3.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="790"></p><ul><li><p>Put the bottom left tail of the First set of strings</p><strong><p>over</p></strong><p>the “U”</p></li><li><p>Put top left of the “U”</p><strong><p>underneath</p></strong><p>the loop of the First set.</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-4.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-5.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Using the top right tail of the “U” set, you’re going to weave</p><strong><p>over, under, and over</p></strong><p>the loops. See below!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-6.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="761"></p><ul><li><p>Next you’ll pull the ends to tighten the knot. Use your fingers to manipulate the yarn and make it pretty!</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-7.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-8.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-22.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-21.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Now it’s time to cut the ends of each side and make them even.</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-9.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-10.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-23.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="103"></p><ul><li><p>Place the four strings of one side next to one another.</p></li><li><p>Do a quick stitch through them with needle and thread to hold them together.</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-11.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-12.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Place Fold Over Elastic (FOE) on top, wrong side down, and pin. I cut about 6 inches of elastic (knowing I’d lose a little of the length during stitching) but it was just a smidge too much. Next time I’ll do 5 inches. You may even want to do just 4 inches to make it snugger. It’s up to you!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-13.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="653"></p><ul><li><p>Use sewing machine or thread and needle and same colored thread to stitch on FOE. I used red so you could see what I was doing!</p></li><li><p>Snip off excess.</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-14.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-15.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><ul><li><p>Place unfinished headband around head and see if the elastic will be tight enough. If not, snip a little off before sewing it to the other side.</p></li><li><p>Stitch across other side of strings to hold together.</p></li><li><p>Sew FOE to yarn.</p></li></ul><figure id="attachment_1949" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-1949" class="post__figure"><img class="size-medium post__image" src="./_images/diy-knotted-headband-16.jpg" alt="DIY Knotted Headband Tutorial by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="637" height="423"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-1949"><p>My sewing machine was bird-nesting today. Very annoying. Just ignore that!</p></figcaption></figure><ul><li><p>Because of the bird-nesting (and the red thread I used for you to see!), these DIY photos aren’t the prettiest! However, I assure you if you use matching thread and your machine isn’t acting up, this DIY is both easy and really nice looking!</p></li></ul><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
