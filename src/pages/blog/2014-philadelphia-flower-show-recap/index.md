<p>We spent the</p><strong><p>entire day</p></strong><p>yesterday {read: 6+ hours} at the</p><a title="The Flower Show" href="http://theflowershow.com/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"><strong><p>Philadelphia Flower Show</p></strong></a><p>(and yes, I</p><strong><p>*did*</p></strong><p>in fact paint my nails to look like little butterfly wings for the occasion!). It was a wonderful date, as usual, and we took tons and tons of photos. Here’s just a glimpse of what we saw!</p><p>Every year since we started dating, the Husband and I have an annual Flower Show date, where we spend a million hours wandering around the showroom floor, buy plants that we inevitably kill only a couple months later, and then go get dinner. It’s a tradition that I’ve come to really look forward to. This year was no exception, with a fun theme of</p><strong><p>ARTiculture</p></strong><p>. Designers, florists, gardeners and even students created beautiful works of art based on the masters, but recreated them with flowers. So wonderful. I, the art major, was especially excited for this. We even went in the Make &#x26; Take Room where I crafted a flower fascinator to wear for the duration of the day, and the Butterfly Experience Room where pretty bugs flew on my head and neck and gave me goosebumps. We collectively took over 300 photos, but I won’t bore you with all of them.
