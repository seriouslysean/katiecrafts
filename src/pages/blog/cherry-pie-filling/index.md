<p>Recipe: Cherry Pie Filling</p><p>A few weeks ago, I stole a good amount of sour cherries from my Dad’s cherry tree. I put them to great use last weekend when I made homemade cherry pie filling for some cherry tarts! Next week, I will share the very easy no-bake recipe for those, but today it’s all about the filling!</p><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2165.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2166.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><p>Fact: I don’t like cherries. I never order cherry pie or cherry flavored things, but I wanted to make a red, white and blue dessert for 4th of July and I really wanted to use Dad’s fresh cherries to do it. When I tried the filling, I just about died. It’s so delicious! Turns out I</p><em><p>do</p></em><p>like cherries, but only if they are fresh! I hope you’ll love this recipe too!</p><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2167.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2168.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><h2>Ingredients:</h2><ul><li><p>6 cups of pitted tart cherries</p></li><li><p>2/3 cup water</p></li><li><p>2 Tablespoons fresh lemon juice</p></li><li><p>1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract</p></li><li><p>4 Tablespoons cornstarch</p></li><li><p>2/3 cup granulated white sugar</p></li></ul><h2>Instructions:</h2><div class="post__gallery"><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2466.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2467.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div><div class="post__gallery-item">
          <img class="post__gallery-image post__image" src="./_images/img-2473.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; https://www.katiecrafts.com" width="600" height="400">
        </div></div><p>Before you begin, pit your cherries! This part was time consuming, extremely messy (we’re talking cherry juice flying every which way!- wear gloves!!), but pretty easy to do. You just push a disposable straw through the top of the cherry and pop the pit out of the bottom. Easy peasy.</p><ul><li><p>Add ALL of your ingredients to a large pot on medium heat. Stir together.</p></li></ul><p>It looks so gross in the beginning!</p><ul><li><p>Bring to boil. Reduce heat and let simmer for 10 minutes until thickened. STIR CONSTANTLY, making sure it doesn’t burn on the bottom of the pan.</p></li></ul><p>Ahh, turning into a nice syrupy mix now!</p><ul><li><p>Remove from heat and let cool to room temperature.</p></li><li><p>Enjoy the most wonderful cherry pie filling you’ve ever had!</p></li></ul><p><img class="size-full post__image" src="./_images/img-2509.jpg" alt="Cherry Pie Filling Recipe by Katie Crafts; http://www.katiecrafts.com" width="980" height="980"></p><h2>Tips:</h2><ul><li><p>Refrigerating the filling will turn it into a gelatinous jelly mass, which is totally fine but looks gross. Simply reheat in a pan over medium heat until the filling is back to a syrupy consistency. Then bring it back to room temp before using.</p></li><li><p>There is cornstarch in this recipe, so it cannot be frozen.</p></li></ul><p>What is your favorite cherry recipe?</p>
